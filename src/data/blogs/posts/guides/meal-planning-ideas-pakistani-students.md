Being a student in Pakistan is a balancing act. You're trying to pass your exams, keep up with your social life, and somehow not go bankrupt by ordering "Zingers" every second night. The "Mess Food" is often hit-or-miss, and the "Dhaba" downstairs is a recipe for a stomach upset during finals week.

The solution? **Meal Planning.** It sounds like something "Mummies" do, but for a student, it’s a survival skill. It saves you money, prevents the "What should I eat?" stress, and keeps your energy levels stable.

## 1. The 7-Day "Hostel Survival" Menu (Rs. 2,000 Budget)
If you have access to a small induction stove or a shared kitchen, this is your blueprint.

| Day | Breakfast | Lunch (at Campus) | Dinner (Hostel) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mon** | Boiled Egg + Paratha | Fruit & Nut mix | Daal Chawal (Bulk Cooked) |

## 2. Breakfast: The "Brain Fuel" Phase
Skip the sugary cereals. They lead to a 10 AM crash during your most important lecture.
*   **The Power of Dates:** Instead of sugar, add two "Aseel" dates to your oatmeal or yogurt. It’s natural energy.
*   **The "Boiled Egg" Hack:** If you have an electric kettle, you can actually boil eggs in it. Just put them in cold water, bring to a boil, and turn it off. Let them sit for 10 minutes. 

## 3. Lunch: The "Campus Snack" Strategy
Avoid the canteen fried food. It makes you sleepy (Food Coma) in the afternoon.
*   **Roasted Chickpeas (Bhuna Chana):** Buy a 1kg bag from the local Kiryana store. It’s the highest protein-to-price ratio food in Pakistan. Keep a small bag in your backpack.
*   **Fruit Rotation:** Buy seasonal. In winter, Kinnows are cheap and full of Vitamin C. In summer, bananas are your best friend for instant energy.

## 4. Dinner: The "Bulk Cooking" Principle
Cooking every night is impossible. Cook **Twice a week.**
*   **The Base:** Cook a large pot of Daal (Lentils) or Aloo Matar (Potato Peas) on Sunday night.
*   **Variation:** 
    *   Night 1: Eat with Roti.
    *   Night 2: Mix it with rice (Daal Chawal).
    *   Night 3: Mash it and put it inside a toasted sandwich. 
    It’s the same base, but it feels like three different meals.

## 5. Shopping Hacks for 2025
*   **Buy Loose:** Don't buy branded, packaged pulses (daal). Buy them "Khula" from the wholesaler. You'll save Rs. 50-80 per kg.
*   **The "End of Day" Discount:** Visit the Sabzi Mandi or local vegetable vendor at 8 PM. They often lower prices for whatever is left so they don't have to carry it back.

## 6. How to Plan Without a Fridge?
Many hostel rooms don't have fridges. How do you survive?
*   **Non-Perishables are King:** Lentils, Rice, Potatoes, Onions, and Garlic don't need a fridge.
*   **The "One-Meal" Rule:** If you cook meat, finish it in one go or share with a roommate. Never keep cooked meat out overnight in the Pakistani heat.
*   **Egg Life:** Eggs stay fresh for 7-10 days at room temperature if it’s not peak July heat.

## 7. Hydration: The Hidden Nutritionist
We often mistake "Thirst" for "Hunger." You think you need a Rs. 200 snack, but you actually just need 2 glasses of water.
*   **The Bottle Rule:** Keep a 1L bottle on your desk. If it’s empty by 12 PM, you’re doing great. If it’s still full at 5 PM, your brain is dehydrated.

## Final Word
Nutrition isn't about "Fancy Salads." It’s about not letting your blood sugar drop to the point where you reach for a packet of biscuits. Manage your meals like you manage your syllabus—one small, planned step at a time.
