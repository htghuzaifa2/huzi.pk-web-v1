With the implementation of Safe City projects across Pakistan, traffic monitoring has gone digital. Gone are the days when you only got a ticket if a warden stopped you. Today, cameras in Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad are constantly monitoring speed, seatbelts, and red lights. You might have a "Challan" pending against your vehicle without even knowing it—and letting it sit unpaid can lead to heavy fines or trouble when selling your car.

Here is the 2025 guide to checking and paying your e-challans nationwide.

## 📍 1. Lahore & Punjab (The Most Advanced System)
The Punjab Safe Cities Authority (PSCA) has the most integrated e-challan system in the country.

*   **Official Portal:** [echallan.psca.gop.pk](https://echallan.psca.gop.pk)
*   **How to Check:** Enter your **Vehicle Number** (e.g., LEC-20-1234) and your **CNIC**.
*   **How to Pay:**
    1.  Generate a **PSID** (Payment System Identifier) from the portal.
    2.  Use **ATM, Mobile Banking (JazzCash/EasyPaisa), or Over-the-Counter** at any bank. 
    3.  On EasyPaisa: Go to 'More' -> 'Govt Payments' -> 'Punjab e-Challan'.

## 📍 2. Islamabad (Federal Capital)
Islamabad traffic police uses the "Safe City" camera network to monitor violations across the capital.

*   **Official Portal:** [ictpolice.gov.pk/echallan](https://ictpolice.gov.pk/echallan/)
*   **Alternative:** Download the **"City Islamabad"** App (Available on iOS/Android). It lets you check for violations using your registration number.
*   **How to Pay:** 
    1.  Challans can be paid through the **City Islamabad app** directly.
    2.  Via **NBP (National Bank of Pakistan)** branches. 
    3.  Digital payments are now supported through the **ICT Police portal** using Kredit/Debit cards.

## 📍 3. Karachi & Sindh
Sindh has recently revamped its digital traffic management system. While not yet as automated as Lahore, the tracking is active.

*   **Official Portal:** [echallan.sindhpolice.gov.pk](https://echallan.sindhpolice.gov.pk)
*   **How to Check:** You will need your **Registration Number** and **Chassis Number** (found on your vehicle's registration book/card).
*   **Wait and See:** In many cases in Karachi, challans are still sent to the physical address registered with the Excise department. Ensure your home address is updated in their records.
*   **How to Pay:** Paid via **National Bank (NBP)** or through selected digital wallets like **EasyPaisa** under the 'Sindh Traffic Fine' section.

## ⚙️ General Process: How to Never Miss a Payment

Regardless of your city, follow this routine once every 3 months:

1.  **Check Every Quarter:** Sites don't always send SMS notifications. Make it a habit to check the portal every 90 days.
2.  **Verify the Evidence:** Official portals usually provide a **photograph or video snippet** of the violation. Check this to ensure it was actually your vehicle (sometimes number plates are misread).
3.  **Pay Within 10 Days:** Some provinces offer a "early bird" grace period, while others apply a surcharge if not paid within 15 days.
4.  **Keep the PDF:** Digital receipts are valid. Save a screenshot of the "Paid" status as it can take 24-48 hours for the system to update across all databases.

## 📱 Paying via Mobile Apps (JazzCash / EasyPaisa)
This is the "Hostel Hack" for students and busy professionals. You don't need to visit a bank.
1.  Log in to **EasyPaisa / JazzCash**.
2.  Go to **'Govt. Payments'**.
3.  Select your province (Punjab, Sindh, or ICT).
4.  Enter the **Challan ID** or **Vehicle Number**.
5.  Pay instantly using your balance. The status updates in the police system within 24 hours.

## ⚠️ Warning: The "Ghost" Challan
If you are buying a used car, **always check for e-challans first**. Many sellers hide the fact that the vehicle has thousands of rupees in unpaid fines. When the ownership transfers to you, those fines become your responsibility. Simply enter the car's plate number on the portals above before handing over the cash.
