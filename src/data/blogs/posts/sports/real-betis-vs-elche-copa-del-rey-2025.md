Under the grand arch of Seville’s La Cartuja stadium, a story of two identities unfolded: the established ambition of Real Betis against the resilient spirit of Elche CF. The final whistle blew on a 2-1 victory for Real Betis, but the scoreline cannot capture the symphony of emotions played out over 97 minutes.

## A Rivalry of Fine Margins
Across 17 matches, the record is incredibly tight. Real Betis holds 7 wins to Elche’s 6, with 4 draws. Historically, Betis has scored 26 goals to Elche’s 21. When these teams meet, form guides flutter out the window, replaced by passion and pride.

## The Match Unfolded
The game was a tactical duel between Manuel Pellegrini and Elche's Eder Sarabia.

### First Half: A Cautious Stalemate
Both teams probed cautiously. Elche's compact 4-5-1 formation stifled Betis's creative hubs. The best chance fell to Giovani Lo Celso, whose free-kick was parried by Matias Dituro.

### Second Half: The Explosion of Drama
In the 58th minute, Elche struck first through defender Léo Petrot. Seeing his team falter, Pellegrini made a decisive triple change, introducing Pablo Fornals and Chimy Ávila.

The momentum shifted in the 68th minute when Ávila fired in a devastating equaliser. Just twelve minutes later, Ávila completed his fairytale, firing home the winning goal in the 80th minute.

## Tactical Keys and Key Players
*   **The Super-Sub Effect:** Chimy Ávila's clinical double was the single biggest factor in the result.
*   **Possession vs. Precision:** Elche dominated the ball (62.3%), but Betis demonstrated more cutting edge, making their shots count.
*   **Aitor Ruibal (Betis):** A tireless force who provided the crucial assist for the winning goal.
*   **Nelson Deossa (Betis):** Earned praise for his physical presence in midfield.

## What This Result Means
For Real Betis, this quarter-final ticket demonstrates the character needed to compete on multiple fronts. For Elche, there is immense pride; they went toe-to-toe with a top-tier side and earned the respect of everyone watching.
