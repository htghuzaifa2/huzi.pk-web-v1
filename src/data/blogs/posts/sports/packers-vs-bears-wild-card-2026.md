Yaar, if you've ever watched a match where hope seemed lost, and your team rose from the ashes—that's exactly what happened in Chicago on **January 10, 2026**.

The **Chicago Bears** pulled off the largest comeback in franchise playoff history, erasing an **18-point deficit** to defeat the Green Bay Packers 31-27. 

## The Quick Highlights: The Windy City Miracle

| Metric | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Final Score** | Bears 31, Packers 27 |
| **Large Deficit** | 21-3 at halftime (18 points) |
| **The Winning Play** | **Caleb Williams** 25-yard TD to **DJ Moore** (1:43 left) |
| **The Closer** | **Jaquan Brisker** deflected Jordan Love's final pass |
| **Bears History** | First playoff win since 2010 |

## How the Drama Unfolded: A Tale of Two Halves

The first half belonged to Green Bay. **Jordan Love**, fresh off concussion protocol, sliced through the Bears to build a 21-3 lead. It felt like the old days of Packers dominance.

But the second half was different. Coach **Ben Johnson** rallied his team, and the defense clamped down, forcing four straight punts. 
*   **The Comeback:** **D'Andre Swift** powered in a 5-yard TD; **Olamide Zaccheaus** caught an 8-yarder.
*   **The Turning Point:** Packers kicker **Brandon McManus** missed a 44-yard field goal.
*   **The Dagger:** Williams engineered a 66-yard drive, culminating in the winner to Moore.

## The Rivalry Reshaped

This oldest feud (since 1921) has seen eras of dominance. Packers ruled recent years, but the Bears taking 3 of the last 5 meetings signals a shift. **Caleb Williams**, the No. 1 pick, proved he is built for big moments.
